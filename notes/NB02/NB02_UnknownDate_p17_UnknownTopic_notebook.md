## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page documents a **factory tour or educational video about LEGO's injection moulding manufacturing process**, likely taken during a visit to or presentation about a LEGO production facility. The notes follow the physical flow of production from raw material to finished product storage.

**The narrative arc of the page follows the production sequence:**

1. **Raw material input:** Plastic granulate is stored in silos as neutral (uncoloured) granular material.
2. **Colour injection:** 50 different colours can be injected at the moulding stage, reducing the risk of having wrong colours mixed in. Colour is added at the point of moulding rather than pre-mixed.
3. **Moulding:** Injection moulding occurs at 29,000 PSI. A key efficiency note — cycle time has been reduced by 1 second to 12 seconds total. The TIP 2015 initiative introduced standardisation, larger machines, and a shift from hydraulic to electric presses.
4. **Mould specificity:** Each mould is dedicated to producing only one type of brick — highlighting the precision and scale of LEGO's tooling investment.
5. **Sustainability:** Plastic waste is ground down and reused, reflecting environmental responsibility in the process.
6. **Low volume / automation:** Low volume moulding lines can run without human operators, pointing toward automation as a strategic direction. The goal is to reduce mould costs through R&D experimentation.
7. **Logistics:** ABVs (Automated Box Vehicles / AGVs) take completed boxes to storage, with barcodes tracking each box.

**The right page** extends the story into quality control, scale statistics (½ billion units per year), packing line throughput (50,000 in 24 hours), and international production (Jiaxing, China).

The visual layout reinforces the **process flow metaphor** — the pipeline illustration at the top literally connects the stages, and the eye is guided downward through the production sequence. Cloud bubbles group related concepts, while bold banners highlight key process names. The combination of flow diagrams, data callouts, and annotated illustrations makes this a highly effective process documentation sketchnote.
