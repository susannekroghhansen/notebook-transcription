This page documents key findings from a **UX Maturity Research** study, specifically focusing on the **Discovery phase** of design work. The notes read as synthesised insights — likely gathered from interviews or surveys with stakeholders including Product Managers (PMs) and design team members.

**The central argument of the page** is a tension between two realities:
- **What should happen:** Designers should be involved early, conducting discovery to define the problem space before solutions are pursued.
- **What actually happens:** Product teams often skip discovery and jump straight to solution mode; PMs report not seeing much discovery activity; and design is sometimes perceived as a bottleneck rather than an enabler.

The bullet points build a coherent narrative arc:
1. **Aspiration** — Designers should be engaged as early as possible.
2. **Reality gap** — Discovery is not always used; teams default to solution mode.
3. **Perceived value** — When design IS involved early, it is valued for scoping and defining the opportunity/problem space.
4. **Stakeholder perception** — PMs notice the absence of discovery.
5. **Specific desire** — Stakeholders want designers to explore UX possibilities within SAP (suggesting an enterprise software context).
6. **Practical contribution** — Designers should help establish the "As Is" picture (current state mapping).
7. **Friction point** — Design is sometimes seen as a bottleneck, which likely contributes to teams bypassing discovery.
