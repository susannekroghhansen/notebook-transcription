## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page is a sketchnote documenting a visit to (or presentation about) a **LEGO manufacturing facility**, specifically focused on the **Moulding Team** and the broader factory operations. The page captures several interconnected themes:

### Production Scale
The staggering scale of LEGO production is a recurring emphasis: **½ billion minifigures per year** (described as "the largest population in the world" — a playful and memorable framing), **50,000 boxes packed in 24 hours**, and a **39-metre-high warehouse**. These facts are consistently placed inside cloud bubbles, a visual device that signals "key information" or "things to remember."

### Process Flow
The page traces a loose manufacturing and logistics flow: moulding (with instructions for temperature and pressure) → quality department (measuring) → counting machines → paper bags → packing lines (semi-automated and automated). The arrow from "Counting Machines" to "Paper Bag" is the most explicit process-flow element, but the spatial arrangement of clouds broadly follows a top-to-bottom, left-to-right logic that mirrors the production sequence.

### Infrastructure
The HIGH BAY warehouse illustration anchors the right side of the page, visually communicating the physical enormity of the storage infrastructure. The annotation "39m HIGH" reinforces this. The warehouses are depicted as towering shelf structures, making the scale tangible.

### Technology Levels
The distinction between **semi-automated** and **automated** packing lines is explicitly called out, with the automated lines receiving a prominent banner treatment at the bottom. The "Robots / Humidity / Air / More space" cloud suggests the automated environment has specific technical and environmental requirements.

### Global Context
The bottom note about **Jiaxing, China** ("2 hours from Shanghai, models the new factories") adds a global manufacturing dimension — this facility in China is positioned as a template or benchmark for future LEGO factories.

### Visual Rhetoric
The use of cloud/thought-bubble shapes throughout creates a sense of these being "ideas" or "highlights" captured in the moment — consistent with sketchnote methodology where clouds often denote key concepts. The page communicates enthusiasm and information density simultaneously, typical of a factory tour or briefing session.

## 4. VISUAL STYLE & COMPOSITION

### Medium
Black fineliner/rollerball pen for all linework and text, with **grey marker or pencil shading** used to add depth to cloud bubbles (shadow effect on lower edges) and to the warehouse shelf illustrations (hatching on alternate shelves). A **brown/tan tone** is used on the title banner fold. The overall palette is **monochromatic with warm accent**, consistent with a two-tool sketchnote approach.

### Typography
Mixed hand-lettering styles throughout:
- **Large bold block capitals** for section headers: "HIGH BAY," "WAREHOUSES," "COUNTING MACHINES," "AUTOMATED PACKING LINES"
- **Mixed case flowing script** for body notes within clouds
- **Consistent pen weight** with slightly heavier strokes on headers for hierarchy
- Numbers are emphasised with underlines (e.g., "50,000" double-underlined)

### Composition
- **Top-left to bottom-right diagonal flow** — the eye is drawn from the title banner (top-left) across the mould instructions cloud, down through the quality and minifig clouds, and finally to the bottom text about Jiaxing
- **Right column** is reserved for the warehouse/infrastructure visual, creating a clear spatial separation between process information (left/centre) and physical infrastructure (right)
- **Cloud bubbles** of varying sizes create visual rhythm and hierarchy — larger clouds for more important facts
- **Boxed labels** (Counting Machines, Paper Bag) contrast with clouds to signal process steps vs. conceptual notes
- The page is **densely packed** but not chaotic — spatial grouping and consistent use of cloud shapes maintain legibility

### Overall Aesthetic
The page has the energetic, information-rich character of a **live sketchnote taken during a factory tour or presentation**. It prioritises capturing key facts and relationships over decorative polish. The dot-grid background (visible through the white page) provides subtle structure. The style is functional visual thinking — practical, fast, and effective at communicating complex manufacturing information at a glance.
