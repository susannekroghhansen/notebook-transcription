## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page documents **LEGO's retail distribution channels and partner ecosystem**, likely from a business lecture, workshop, or case study presentation. The page is structured into three logical zones:

The clouds visually signal "categories" or "types" — a common sketchnote convention for grouping related concepts. The numbers (250, 760) indicate the scale of each format globally.

**Zone 2 (Middle): The Partner Channel**
The page distinguishes between two types of retail partners: large-format retailers (super/hypermarkets) and small independent stores (mom & pop). The curved arrow bridges this general categorisation to the specific named partners: Walmart, Target, Amazon, and LEGO Retail. The "BIG 3" label (in a circle, top-right of the box) likely refers to Walmart, Target, and Amazon as the dominant US retail partners. The statistic "90% SALES IN THE US" — circled for emphasis — underscores the critical importance of the US market and these three partners. The drop-ship cloud adds a contextual note about COVID-era logistics: rather than shipping from LEGO's own warehouse, product was sourced directly from Amazon's warehouse.

**Zone 3 (Bottom): European Distribution Complexity**
The Europe section introduces a tiered partner system, where each country manages its own partners. Tiers 1–3 are prioritised; Tier 4 (associated with mom & pop stores) may not receive their orders. The note "every night we reschedule" with the arrow to "Bad customer experience" highlights an operational pain point — nightly rescheduling of allocations leads to poor outcomes for lower-tier partners. The moon/stars doodle reinforces the "every night" concept visually.

**Overall Argument:** The page communicates that LEGO's distribution is complex, multi-tiered, and heavily US-centric, with structural challenges (especially in Europe) that create customer experience problems at the lower tiers of the partner hierarchy.
