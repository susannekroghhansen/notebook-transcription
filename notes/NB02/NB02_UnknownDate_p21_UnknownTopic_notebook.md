The DC icon with "G shipping to all plants globally" establishes the macro context: a global distribution centre is responsible for shipping to all plants worldwide. The scanner icon on the right suggests that incoming goods or shipments are tracked/scanned, possibly at a receiving dock. The word "transnational" reinforces the international scope of operations.

This is a significant operational concept (likely reducing handling costs or time).

**Layer 3 — Silo Filling & Supply Questions (Bottom)**
The three silos represent physical storage infrastructure. The annotations clarify that silos are "filled by external suppliers" via "bulk shipment of raw material" — meaning the raw material arrives in large quantities directly from outside vendors. The trailing question "How do we determin[e]..." at the bottom-left suggests an open question, possibly about how fill levels, reorder points, or supplier allocations are determined — indicating this is an **active working session** capturing both facts and unresolved questions.

**Overall Argument:** The page documents a logistics model where raw materials are shipped in bulk from external suppliers directly into silos at global plants, with a distribution centre coordinating global shipping. The sketchnote captures both the process and an emerging analytical question about decision-making within that process.
