## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This two-page spread documents a presentation or discussion about **LEGO's global distribution network and supply chain**, with particular focus on the **Asia-Pacific region**.

### Top Page — Global Overview
The top page establishes the big picture: LEGO operates a global network of **sites** (marked on the world map with pins), including a **Global HQ**, **regional hubs** (US/Canada, Europe), **local hubs**, and **factories**. The "LEGO SITES" banner acts as the visual anchor. The Asia annotation on the right clarifies that in Asia, **each country manages its own stock**, with distribution through a **B2B webshop** and **Customer Sales Offices** — a notably decentralised model compared to other regions.

The map communicates geographic reach: LEGO has a presence across all major continents, with pins in the Americas, Africa (South Africa, Dubai), Europe, and Asia.

### Bottom Page — Asia-Pacific Detail & Supply Chain Challenges
The bottom page zooms in on Asia-Pacific, listing specific countries/markets (India, Australia, Malaysia, NZ, China, Korea, Vietnam, Japan). The cluster of pins and the "no free flow" annotation suggest these markets have constrained or controlled stock movement.

The right-side text box flags a **key operational problem**: BTW (likely "between") routing and packing is a bottleneck, particularly because **moulding is a global capacity** and in some cases there is **only one mould** for a product — meaning production cannot simply be redirected.

The central text distinguishes between:
- **Long-term demand planning** (21 months out) — strategic forecasting
- **Short-term demand planning** (3–4 months) — tactical, predicting what is on the market

**Kinaxis** (a supply chain software platform) is mentioned as the tool used for **ICP (Integrated Capacity Planning)**, with the note that while planning "used to be easy," having two factories (focs = factories?) makes stock handling more complex.

The "Right Bricks / Frequency" cloud bubble likely represents the core goal: getting the **right LEGO bricks** to the right place at the right **frequency** — the fundamental supply chain objective.

Overall, the page communicates a tension between LEGO's **global scale** and the **local complexity** of managing stock across many small, independent Asian markets, with supply chain tools and planning horizons being the proposed solutions.
