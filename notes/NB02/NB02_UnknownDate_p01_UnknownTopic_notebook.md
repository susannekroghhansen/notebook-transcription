## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

### Overall Argument
This page documents a proposed **solution framework** for improving how products and teams work together, centred on the concept of **journey-based thinking**. The page moves from problem framing to structural solution to cultural/paradigm shift.

### Logical Flow
1. **The Solution** (top): The framework operates on two levels — *operational* (day-to-day) and *strategic* (elevated, cross-journey). The curly brace makes explicit that both levels serve the same purpose: **bringing together cross-functional stakeholders**. The Venn diagram reinforces this idea of convergence and overlap between groups.

2. **Experience as the Outcome** (upper-right): The circled word "Experience" sits apart as the north star — the reason for doing all of this. It is the desired outcome of the framework.

3. **A Journey Team** (middle): The solution requires a specific team structure — designers and product people with access to business and engineering. This is not a siloed team; it requires cross-disciplinary access. The stacked disc illustration may represent a layered or platform-based team model.

4. **Mindset & Strategy** (middle-right): These two concepts are grouped together and described as setting "the stage for how the products add to the picture." This suggests that before execution, a cultural and strategic foundation must be laid — the *how* of working matters as much as the *what*.

5. **The Problem Being Solved** (lower-left cloud): The thought bubble explicitly names the gap: *"we are not thinking about how users move across products."* This is the core problem — a lack of cross-product journey thinking. The cloud/thought bubble format visually codes this as a current mental model or blind spot, not yet a solution.

6. **A New Paradigm** (bottom): The most visually emphasised phrase on the page. This signals a fundamental shift in thinking, not just a process change. It is paired with "New ways of working" on the right, reinforcing that both mindset and practice must change.

7. **Closing Statements**: The final two lines — *"Value of completion of journeys can be measured"* and *"Connectivity & collaboration"* — serve as grounding principles. They suggest that this new paradigm is not abstract: journeys can be measured, and the mechanism for achieving them is connectivity and collaboration.

### Visual Rhetoric
The page uses **encircling ovals** to mark key concepts (Experience, Mindset/Strategy, New ways of working), **green highlighting** to mark the most important heading (A new paradigm), and a **thought bubble** to visually separate the problem statement from the solution content. The Venn diagram and curly brace work together as logical connectors. The page reads top-to-bottom as a coherent argument: *here is the solution structure → here is the team → here is the mindset needed → here is the problem we're solving → here is the paradigm shift required.*
