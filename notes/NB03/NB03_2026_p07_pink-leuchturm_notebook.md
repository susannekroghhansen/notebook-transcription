## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page is a sketchnote summarising key principles and reflective prompts around **professional networking**. The page communicates a philosophy of networking that is warm, intentional, and human-centred rather than transactional.

**Structural argument of the page:**

- The **title "NETWORKING"** anchors the topic immediately.
- **"5-MIN FAVOUR"** introduces a concept — likely a networking technique where you offer or ask for a small, low-commitment favour as a way to build relationships.
- The surrounding keywords — **"Listening," "Checking," "Coffee," "Tell the Why Behind Well Done," "Brilliant Because"** — are clustered around the central coffee swirl, suggesting these are the *behaviours and conversational tools* that make networking effective. The coffee metaphor implies informal, one-to-one conversations as the primary networking vehicle.
  - *Listening* and *Checking* suggest active, attentive engagement.
  - *Tell the Why Behind Well Done* implies giving meaningful, specific praise rather than generic compliments.
  - *Brilliant Because* reinforces this — a formula for articulate, motivating feedback or appreciation.
- The **central highlighted question** — *"WHAT DO I WANT TO LEARN / WHO COULD HELP ME?"* — reframes networking as a **learning-driven, intentional practice**. Rather than networking for its own sake, the prompt encourages the reader to approach it with clarity of purpose.
- The **two figures with a heart** at the bottom visually resolves the page's message: effective networking is about genuine human connection and warmth.

The light bulb in the corner reinforces the idea that networking is an **insight-generating activity** — it sparks ideas and learning.

Overall, the page argues: *Networking works best when it is intentional (know what you want to learn), human (listen, give meaningful praise, have coffee), and warm (connect genuinely).*

**Overall aesthetic:** Friendly, approachable, and energetic. The style is characteristic of personal sketchnoting — not polished or designed for publication, but expressive, functional, and visually memorable. The use of simple character illustrations with red cheeks gives the page warmth and personality, reinforcing the human connection theme of the content itself.
