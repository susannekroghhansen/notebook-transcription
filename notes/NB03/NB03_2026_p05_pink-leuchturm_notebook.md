## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page is a sketchnote exploring the concept of **confidence gremlins** — the internal voices or fears that undermine self-confidence. The page communicates a nuanced, two-part message:

**Part 1 — What Confidence Gremlins Are:**
The cluster of overlapping lavender bubbles in the upper portion of the page maps out the common **sources or triggers of confidence-undermining thoughts**: age, numbers, presenting, failing, not being smart enough, not being good enough, conflict, seniority, spotlight effect, and fear of being found out (imposter syndrome). These are presented as a tangled, interconnected web — suggesting that these fears overlap and feed into each other rather than existing in isolation.

The **three red-hatched bubbles** (AGE, NUMBERS, NOT GOOD ENOUGH) are visually emphasised, implying they are either the most common, the most powerful, or the ones most specifically highlighted in the source material being noted. The hatching technique draws the eye immediately to these three above all others.

**Part 2 — The Gremlin's Power Is Not Fixed:**
The bottom section delivers the page's central insight. The **Confidence Gremlin** character speaks the words "YOU ARE NOT IN CHARGE" — a declaration that the gremlin is attempting to seize control. However, the surrounding text reframes this: **"THEY CAN BE CRYPTONITE OR A SUPER POWER."** This is the key message — confidence gremlins are not inherently destructive. Depending on how you relate to them, they can either paralyse you (kryptonite) or be harnessed as motivation and self-awareness (super power).

The **two characters** — the gremlin and the rounder, smiling figure — visually represent this duality: the inner critic versus the self. The red explosive burst between them captures the moment of confrontation and choice.

The page number **68** suggests this is part of a longer notebook or course, likely notes from a workshop, book, or coaching session on confidence and inner critic work.

**Typography:** All text is hand-lettered in a casual, clear block-capital style. The title "CONFIDENCE" is notably larger and bolder than the rest, written in red to match the emphasis elements. Body labels are written in a consistent, readable all-caps hand.

**Composition:** The page is divided into two clear zones:
- **Upper two-thirds:** Conceptual mapping — the bubble cluster showing the landscape of confidence fears
- **Lower third:** Narrative/resolution — the character vignette delivering the key takeaway

**Layout style:** Organic and clustered in the upper section (mimicking the tangled nature of self-doubt), transitioning to a more illustrative, scene-based layout at the bottom. The vertical red rule under the title anchors the composition.

**Overall aesthetic:** Energetic, accessible, and slightly playful — typical of sketchnote documentation of personal development or coaching content. The gremlin character adds warmth and humour to what could otherwise be a heavy topic, making the content approachable. The red hatching technique is particularly effective as an emphasis tool, immediately directing attention to the three most important bubble concepts.
