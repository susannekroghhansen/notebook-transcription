## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page presents a **self-coaching framework** structured around three interconnected activities:

### A. Identifying Strengths Through Others' Eyes
The opening question — *"What 3 strengths do you describe me with?"* — is a classic coaching exercise that invites the reader to seek external perspective on their own qualities. The circled "3" focuses the exercise: not an open-ended list, but a constrained, deliberate reflection.

### B. Mapping Values Across a High-Low-Learning Axis
The "VALUES" section, flanked by gem icons, introduces a conceptual framework for understanding one's values as precious and worth excavating. The wavy graph below it visualises a personal journey — the peaks (marked with asterisks as "highs") and valleys (lows) — plotted against a neutral baseline. The axis labels "HIGH / Low / Learning" suggest that this graph tracks not just performance or emotion, but the *learning* dimension of experience. The smiley faces on the vertical axis reinforce an emotional self-assessment reading.

### C. Extracting Insight from Patterns
The prompt *"What do highs have in common / Do it in pairs"* is a coaching technique — examining peak moments to identify recurring themes or strengths. The three-question stack — **WHAT? / SO WHAT? / NOW WHAT?** — is a well-known reflective learning model (Borton's model of reflection), guiding the reader from description → analysis → action.

### D. Personal Strengths Radar
The compass rose with scores (Achievement 8/10, Ideas 9/10, Learn 6/10, Variety 8/10) provides a quantified snapshot of the person's self-assessed strengths profile. The high scores for Ideas and Variety/Achievement suggest a creative, driven personality. The lower score for Learn (6/10) may indicate an area for development or honest self-appraisal.

### Overall Argument
The page argues that **self-coaching is a structured, reflective practice**: ask others for your strengths, map your emotional/performance highs and lows, find patterns in the peaks, and use a simple reflective model (What/So What/Now What) to turn insight into action. The compass rose grounds this in concrete self-knowledge scores.
