## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page documents a personal productivity or self-coaching framework called **"How to Cage Your Gremlins"** — a metaphor drawn from the concept of inner critics or self-sabotaging mental patterns (commonly called "gremlins" in coaching literature, notably Rick Carson's *Taming Your Gremlin*).

### Core Argument
The page teaches a three-step method for managing psychological "gremlins" (self-doubt, fear, inner criticism) through the lens of **Very Small Successes (VSS)**:

1. **Recognize** — Notice when a gremlin is present or when a small success has occurred
2. **Record** — Write it down (in the VSS journal/table shown)
3. **Reflect** — Think about what it means

### Key Reassurance
The note **"You don't have to share them"** positioned beside the VSS table is a significant emotional permission-giving statement — it reassures the reader that these small wins are private, reducing the psychological barrier to acknowledging them honestly.

### The Gremlin Figure
The central ghost/blob figure with its tentative "Yehii" expression embodies the gremlin being "caged" — it looks uncertain rather than threatening, suggesting that once you apply the three R's, the gremlin becomes manageable and even a little pathetic rather than scary.

### WOTW (Win of the Week)
The badge at the bottom introduces a weekly ritual — identifying the single **Win of the Week** — while the question **"Which Gremlin Gets in the way of My work the Most"** grounds the practice in honest self-reflection, linking the positive (wins) with the diagnostic (identifying the dominant gremlin).

### Overall Message
The page argues that small, private, regularly recorded successes are the mechanism by which inner gremlins are "caged" — not by fighting them, but by consistently documenting evidence that contradicts them.
