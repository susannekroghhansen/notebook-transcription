## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page is a structured visual exploration of the concept of **personal strengths**, specifically focusing on three interconnected ideas:

**1. Intent vs. Impact of Strengths (Battery Metaphor)**
The battery illustration at the top is a powerful central metaphor: strengths are like a battery — they can give energy (upward arrows, intent) or drain energy (downward arrows, impact). The split within the battery suggests that what we *intend* to do with our strengths and what *impact* they actually have may differ. The annotation "What Gives You Energy" reinforces that identifying true strengths is fundamentally about energy management.

**2. Energy Audit (Weekly Practice)**
The middle section introduces a practical self-monitoring tool: an **Energy Audit** tracked daily across the week (M–F). The "ea" label in each box and the red stripe suggest logging or rating energy levels each day. The squiggly arrows leading to "When" and "WHAT" prompt the user to reflect on *when* their energy peaks/dips and *what* activities or contexts are responsible — turning raw data into insight.

**3. Actively Stretching Strengths**
The lower section shifts from diagnosis to action. The question "How do I actively STRETCH MY STRENGTHS?" frames the page's ultimate purpose: not just identifying strengths but deliberately developing and deploying them. The five bottom bubbles — **Range, Share, Strategy, Frequency, Complementary** — appear to be five strategic levers or dimensions through which one can stretch a strength:
- *Range*: applying it in broader contexts
- *Share*: teaching or demonstrating it to others
- *Strategy*: using it deliberately and purposefully
- *Frequency*: using it more often
- *Complementary*: pairing it with other strengths

The light bulb (ideas) and spiral/target (strategy) icons reinforce the move from reflection to purposeful action.

**Overall narrative arc of the page:** Identify what gives you energy → Audit your energy daily → Reflect on when and what → Actively stretch your strengths using five dimensions.
