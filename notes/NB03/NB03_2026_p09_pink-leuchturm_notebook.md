## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This double-page spread is a personal visual reflection on a **non-linear, "squiggly" career journey** — a concept popularised by career coach Helen Tupper and Sarah Ellis. The central metaphor is explicit: the thick, winding black line represents a career that does not travel in a straight line but loops, backsides, and curves through many different skills, roles, and experiences.

**The four red thematic anchors** frame the entire spread as a coherent narrative:
- *Following My Passions* and *Supported By My Leaders* (running vertically on the right) establish the **enabling conditions** of this career
- *Taking Chances* (upper page) and *Being Brave / Experimenting* (lower page) describe the **mindset and actions** taken

The **skill tags** attached to the squiggly line — spanning project coordination, digital knowledge, product development, communication, facilitation, UX design, web design, strategic design, and visual thinking — represent the diverse, cross-disciplinary nature of the author's experience. Rather than a linear progression, these skills are encountered at different points along a winding path, reinforcing the "squiggly" metaphor.

The **character figure** at the start of the line humanises the journey and positions the author as the protagonist of their own career story.

The **cloud bubbles** at the bottom shift the tone from retrospective to prospective: *Finding My People* suggests that community and belonging are valued outcomes, while *I Wonder What's Next* closes the spread with open curiosity rather than a fixed destination — perfectly consistent with the squiggly career philosophy.

Overall, the page communicates: *"My career has been winding and varied, driven by passion and bravery, and I'm excited about what comes next."*

## 4. VISUAL STYLE & COMPOSITION

**Medium:** Black fineliner/marker (for line work and tags), red marker or brush pen (for titles and clouds), light purple/lavender wash (possibly watercolour or light marker, used sparingly for background shading behind the squiggly line)

**Colour Palette:**
- **Black** — dominant; used for the squiggly line, character, tag outlines, and small icons
- **Red/coral** — used exclusively for all thematic titles and headings (*Taking Chances*, *Being Brave*, *Experimenting*, *Following My Passions*, *Supported By My Leaders*, cloud outlines, *Finding My People*, *I Wonder What's Next*), creating strong visual hierarchy
- **White/cream** — the dot-grid notebook paper background
- **Lavender/light purple** — subtle wash behind the squiggly line, adding dimensionality

**Typography:**
- Titles are hand-lettered in a bold, casual all-caps style with slight variation in letter size for energy
- Tag labels are written in a smaller, slightly more controlled hand
- The black banner "MY SQUIGGLY CAREER…" uses a white-on-black reverse treatment for contrast

**Composition:**
- The spread is **landscape-oriented** (the notebook is turned sideways), allowing the squiggly line to travel across a wide horizontal canvas
- The red vertical text on the right margin acts as a **spine or anchor** for the whole composition
- The layout is **organic and non-grid-based**, mirroring the squiggly career concept itself
- Tags are distributed fairly evenly across both pages, with the clouds grounding the lower page
- The character figure provides a clear **visual entry point** in the upper left

**Overall Aesthetic:** Playful, personal, and energetic — characteristic of sketchnote/visual journaling style. The looseness of the line work feels intentional and expressive rather than rough. The limited colour palette (black + red + lavender) keeps the spread visually coherent despite its dynamic, non-linear composition.
