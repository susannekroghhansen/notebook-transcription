## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page communicates a two-part message about career exploration and possibility thinking.

**Upper section — Mindmapping as a tool for exploring possibilities:**
The "MINDMAPPING MOVES" banner introduces the concept of using mind mapping as a method for career thinking. The large thought cloud contains a central tangled node (representing a current situation or central idea) with four directional arrows branching outward, each labelled with a different *type* of move* or *mode of thinking*:
- **Dream** — aspirational, imaginative thinking
- **Obvious** — the conventional or expected path (with a looping line suggesting it circles back, possibly critiquing it as circular or limited)
- **Pivot** — a lateral or redirectional career move
- **Ambitious** — a bold, stretch-goal direction

Together, these four labels suggest a framework for mind-mapping one's career options: you can dream, take the obvious route, pivot, or be ambitious. The arrows and central tangle imply that all these directions radiate from the same starting point — your current situation.

**Lower section — A simple, actionable habit:**
The stick figure and speech bubble deliver a concrete, human-scale action: *"Have one curious career conversation a month."* This grounds the abstract mind-mapping framework in a real-world practice. The word "curious" is key — it frames the conversation as exploratory rather than transactional. The character's cheerful expression reinforces a tone of openness and positivity.

**Overall argument:** Career exploration ("Possibilities") doesn't require grand gestures — it can be approached through structured imagination (mindmapping with distinct move-types) and simple recurring habits (one conversation a month). The page moves from concept (top) to action (bottom), mirroring a thought-to-behaviour flow.

## 4. VISUAL STYLE & COMPOSITION

**Medium:** Black fineliner/pen for outlines and text; red marker for headings, underlines, the speech bubble outline, and hair; grey/lavender watercolour wash or light grey marker for the cloud interior fill and arrow shading.

**Colour palette:** Minimal and deliberate — black, red, and grey/lavender. Red is used consistently as an accent and emphasis colour (titles, underlines, the speech bubble, the figure's hair and mouth), creating visual hierarchy and warmth.

**Typography:** Hand-lettered throughout. The main heading "POSSIBILITIES" uses bold, wide red capitals. "MINDMAPPING MOVES" uses slightly smaller bold black block capitals. Body labels and the speech bubble text use a casual, clear handwritten style with mixed capitalisation.

**Composition:** The page is vertically structured in two clear zones:
1. **Top two-thirds:** Conceptual/diagrammatic — the banner and thought cloud.
2. **Bottom third:** Human/actionable — the character and speech bubble.

This top-to-bottom flow from abstract concept to concrete action is a hallmark of effective sketchnote design.

**Aesthetic character:** Friendly, approachable, and energetic. The loose linework, expressive thought cloud, and simple stick figure give the page a hand-crafted warmth. The red accents prevent the page from feeling cold or overly diagrammatic. The dot-grid notebook background is faintly visible, grounding the page in a bullet-journal or professional notebook context. Page number **71** is visible in the bottom-right corner.
