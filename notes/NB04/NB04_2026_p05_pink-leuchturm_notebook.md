## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page is a sketchnote exploring the concept of **confidence gremlins** — the internal voices and fears that undermine self-confidence. The page makes a clear two-part argument:

**Part 1 — Naming the Gremlins:**
The bubble cluster in the upper portion catalogues common confidence-eroding thoughts and triggers: fear of age, numbers/metrics, presenting, failing, not being smart or good enough, conflict, seniority hierarchies, being in the spotlight, and the classic "impostor syndrome" fear of being found out. By placing these fears inside soft, rounded bubbles, the visual design treats them as floating, somewhat insubstantial thoughts — present and numerous, but containable.

Three of the bubbles — **AGE**, **NUMBERS**, and **NOT GOOD ENOUGH** — are singled out with red outlines and heavy red hatching, suggesting these are the most potent or personally resonant gremlins, or perhaps the ones most commonly cited. The hatching creates a visual sense of being "crossed out" or "struck through," which could imply either that these are the most dangerous, or conversely that they are the ones most worth challenging and dismissing.

**Part 2 — Reframing the Gremlin:**
The lower section introduces the **Confidence Gremlin** as a named, personified entity — a small devil figure. Its speech bubble declares "YOU ARE NOT IN CHARGE," which is the gremlin's message to the self: a put-down, a voice of self-doubt. However, the key reframe comes in the red explosion text: **"THEY CAN BE CRYPTONITE OR A SUPER POWER."** This is the central insight of the page — that confidence gremlins are not inherently destructive. The choice of how to relate to them determines their power. The smiling figure to the right of the gremlin visually embodies this positive possibility.

The page as a whole communicates a **coaching or self-development insight**: awareness of your gremlins is the first step; the second is choosing whether to let them be your kryptonite (weakness) or reframe them as a superpower (motivator, source of empathy, driver of growth).

## 4. VISUAL STYLE & COMPOSITION

**Medium:** Black fineliner/pen for text and characters; lavender/purple marker for bubble shapes; red marker for title, emphasis hatching, bubble outlines, and the explosive border.

**Colour palette:** Minimal — white dotted notebook paper background, black ink, soft lavender/purple, and bold red. The red is used exclusively for emphasis and energy, making it a strong visual signal throughout the page.

**Typography:** All text is hand-lettered in block/print capitals. The title "CONFIDENCE" is notably larger and bolder than the body text, drawn in red. Body text within bubbles is in smaller black print capitals, consistent in size and weight. The lower section text ("YOU ARE NOT IN CHARGE," "THEY CAN BE CRYPTONITE OR A SUPER POWER") uses slightly larger, bolder lettering for impact.

**Composition:** The page follows a clear top-to-bottom narrative flow:
- Title → Trigger cloud → Gremlin character + reframe message
- The bubble cluster occupies roughly the top 60% of the page; the character illustration and key message occupy the bottom 40%.
- The red zigzag explosion border at the bottom acts as a visual full stop, anchoring the page's conclusion with maximum energy.

**Notebook format:** Dot-grid bullet journal or sketchnote notebook (dots visible in the background). A vertical black line on the left margin suggests a margin rule. The page is slightly warped from scanning, indicating a physical bound notebook.

**Overall aesthetic:** Energetic, accessible, and slightly playful — characteristic of coaching/personal development sketchnotes. The gremlin character adds humour and approachability to what could otherwise be an anxiety-inducing topic. The red hatching and explosion border create urgency and emphasis without overwhelming the clean, open layout.
