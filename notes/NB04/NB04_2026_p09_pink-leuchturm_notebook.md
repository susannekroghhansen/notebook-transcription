## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This two-page sketchnote is a personal visual autobiography of a professional career — specifically framed through the concept of the **"squiggly career"**, a term popularised in career development thinking to describe non-linear, multi-directional professional paths rather than traditional ladder-style progression.

### Core Narrative

The squiggly line is the central metaphor: a career that winds, loops back, spirals, and takes unexpected turns rather than moving in a straight upward trajectory. The character at the start (page 72, top left) represents the author at the beginning of their journey, and the line traces outward through a series of roles and skill areas — from **Project Coordinator** and **Manager** through **Product Development**, **Communication**, **Digital Knowledge**, into **Facilitation**, **Strategy**, **Web Design**, **UX Design**, **Visual Thinking**, and ultimately **Strategic Design**.

### Thematic Framing

The right-margin title — **"Following My Passions / Supported by My Leaders"** — provides the interpretive frame for the entire spread. The career journey is not presented as accidental or chaotic, but as passion-driven and enabled by supportive leadership. This reframes the squiggly line from something potentially anxiety-inducing (career uncertainty) into something positive and intentional.

The large red text on each page reinforces this:
- **"TAKING CHANCES"** (page 72) — the upper half of the journey is characterised by risk-taking and experimentation
- **"BEING BRAVE / EXPERIMENTING"** (page 73) — the lower half continues this theme, emphasising courage and a willingness to try new things

### The Cloud Bubbles as Reflection Points

The two thought clouds at the bottom of page 73 shift the tone from retrospective to prospective. **"FINDING MY PEOPLE"** suggests that community and belonging have become important values in the career journey. **"I WONDER WHAT'S NEXT"** ends the spread on a note of open curiosity — the squiggly line is not finished; the career continues.

### Overall Argument

The page communicates that a non-linear career built on passion, bravery, and experimentation — and supported by good leadership — is not only valid but rich and fulfilling. The visual language (the looping, energetic line; the cheerful character; the bold red text) reinforces this positive, empowered framing.

## 4. VISUAL STYLE & COMPOSITION

- **Primary line work:** Black fineliner/marker pen (thick, confident strokes for the squiggly line; thinner lines for flags and icons)
- **Text highlights and key words:** Red/coral marker or pen (used for the title, thematic words, and cloud outlines)
- **Character and flag fills:** Some light purple/lavender shading visible inside the squiggly line loops, suggesting a light marker or coloured pencil wash

### Composition

The spread is composed as a **single unified visual field** across both pages, with the squiggly line acting as the connective tissue that links all elements. The composition is deliberately organic and non-grid-aligned — elements are placed where the line takes them, reinforcing the "squiggly" metaphor at the compositional level.

The **right margin** is used as a vertical title strip, with the main heading running sideways — a common sketchnote convention for titling spreads.

The **bottom of page 73** anchors the spread with the two cloud bubbles, providing visual weight at the base and a sense of conclusion/reflection.

### Typography Style

All text is hand-lettered in a casual, energetic style. Key thematic words (**TAKING CHANCES**, **BEING BRAVE**, **EXPERIMENTING**) are written in larger, bolder red lettering with slight variation in letter size for emphasis. Flag labels are written in smaller, neat black capitals. The overall lettering style is expressive but legible, consistent with sketchnote conventions.

### Aesthetic Character

The overall aesthetic is **warm, personal, and playful** — characteristic of reflective sketchnoting used in personal development or career journaling contexts. The style is accessible and human rather than polished or corporate, which reinforces the authenticity of the career story being told.
