## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page communicates a practical framework for **intentional professional networking**, centred on the concept of the "5-minute favour" — small, low-effort acts of generosity that build meaningful professional relationships.

The page is structured around a central question that acts as the strategic anchor:
> *"What do I want to learn? Who could help me?"*

This question, visually emphasised by the large oval highlight and bold red lettering, reframes networking from a transactional or anxiety-inducing activity into a **purposeful, curiosity-driven practice**. The reader is encouraged to approach networking with clarity about their own learning goals first.

The surrounding concepts — **Listening, Checking, Coffee, Tell the Why Behind Well Done, Brilliant Because** — represent specific micro-behaviours or tactics within a networking interaction:

- **Listening** and **Checking** suggest active, attentive engagement during conversations.
- **Coffee** (with its looping connector line) implies that informal one-to-one meetings are the primary context for networking — the loop visually binds all the other concepts together, suggesting coffee chats are the vehicle.
- **Tell the Why Behind Well Done** suggests giving meaningful, specific praise rather than generic compliments — a technique for deepening connection.
- **Brilliant Because** is a related phrase, likely a formula for structured positive feedback (e.g., "That was brilliant *because*...").

The two stick figures with a heart between them at the bottom reinforce the emotional tone: networking is ultimately about **human connection, warmth, and mutual support** — not cold transactional exchange.

The light bulb in the top-left corner frames the whole page as an **ideas/insight section**, signalling that this is actionable thinking rather than passive note-taking.
