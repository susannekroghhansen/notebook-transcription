## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This sketchnote summarises the core argument of *The Squiggly Career* by Sarah Ellis. The page communicates a single central thesis: **the traditional, linear career ladder is outdated and should be replaced by a more personal, non-linear ("squiggly") approach to career development.**

The page is structured as a conceptual contrast:

- **Old model (left/upper-right):** The staircase diagram labelled "The Career Ladder" and annotated as "100 years old" and "Assumes Linear" establishes the conventional paradigm. The traditional ladder in the lower-left reinforces this with its rungs labelled Plans, Steps, Destination, Titles, and Promotion — all hallmarks of hierarchical, corporate career thinking.

- **New model (centre/lower-right):** The squiggly speech bubble, the wavy graph, and the phrase "Now careers are personal" introduce the alternative. The key mindset shift is captured in the phrase **"I create not wait"** — a call to agency and proactivity. The "Squiggly Swaps" section proposes replacing old career concepts (Goals → let go of; Plans → wavy/flexible) with new framings: **Milestone, Chapter, Experiment** — language that is iterative, exploratory, and non-hierarchical.

The flowing squiggly line connecting the book cover to the staircase and then looping back through the speech bubble visually enacts the "squiggly" metaphor itself — the page layout *is* the argument.
