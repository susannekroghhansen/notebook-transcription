## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page is a sketchnote about **career exploration and possibility thinking**, structured around two complementary ideas:

**Upper section — "Mindmapping Moves":** The large thought cloud with its central tangle and four radiating arrows represents the act of mind-mapping as a tool for exploring career possibilities. The four labelled directions — *dream*, *obvious*, *pivot*, and *ambitious* — represent different **modes or lenses** through which one might brainstorm career options:
- **Dream** = aspirational, unconstrained thinking
- **Obvious** = the straightforward, expected path
- **Pivot** = a lateral or redirected move
- **Ambitious** = a stretch goal or bold aspiration

The messy central node visually communicates that the starting point of career thinking is often tangled and unclear, and that these four directions are ways to organise or diverge from that complexity. The arrows suggest that all four directions are valid and worth exploring simultaneously.

**Lower section — Action Step:** The stick figure and speech bubble deliver a concrete, actionable takeaway: *"Have one curious career conversation a month."* This grounds the abstract mind-mapping exercise in a simple, recurring habit. The word "curious" is key — it frames the conversations as exploratory rather than transactional, echoing the open-ended spirit of the mind-map above.

**Together**, the page argues: when thinking about career possibilities, use mind-mapping to explore multiple directions (dream, obvious, pivot, ambitious), and then activate those possibilities through regular, curious conversations. The visual language — a dreaming figure, a thought cloud full of branching ideas, and a spoken commitment — creates a narrative arc from inner reflection to outward action.

## 4. VISUAL STYLE & COMPOSITION

**Medium:** Black fineliner/pen for primary drawing and text; red marker or pen for title, box borders, speech bubble border, underlines, and hair/figure accents; grey or blue-grey marker/brush for arrow shading and the thought-cloud shadow fill.

**Colour palette:** Minimal and purposeful — black, red, and grey/lavender. Red is used exclusively for emphasis: the title, the "MINDMAPPING MOVES" box, the "PIVOT" underline, the speech bubble border, and the figure's hair. This creates a clear visual hierarchy where red = important/actionable.

**Typography:** Hand-lettered throughout. The title "POSSIBILITIES" is in large, bold red capitals with a slightly irregular, energetic quality. "MINDMAPPING MOVES" is in clean, even block capitals. The cloud labels mix styles — "PIVOT" in caps, "ambitious" in flowing lowercase, "dream" and "obvious" in casual mixed case — suggesting varying registers or energy levels.

**Composition:** The page is divided into two clear zones: the large thought-cloud occupying the upper ~65% of the page, and the figure-plus-speech-bubble occupying the lower ~35%. This top-heavy layout places the conceptual framework above and the actionable habit below, creating a natural reading flow from abstract to concrete.

**Dot-grid paper:** The underlying dot grid is faintly visible, providing structure without constraining the organic, hand-drawn aesthetic.

**Overall aesthetic:** Warm, approachable, and energetic. The style is characteristic of professional sketchnoting — clean enough to be legible and structured, but loose and playful enough to feel human and generative. The stick figure adds personality and relatability, making the page feel like a personal reflection rather than a formal diagram.
