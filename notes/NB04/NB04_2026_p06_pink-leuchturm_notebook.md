## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page presents a practical self-management framework for dealing with inner "gremlins" — a metaphor commonly used in coaching and personal development for self-sabotaging thoughts, fears, or limiting beliefs.

**Core argument:** The way to "cage" your gremlins is through the disciplined practice of noticing and recording *Very Small Successes (VSS)* — a counter-narrative strategy against negative self-talk.

**The three-R framework** (Recognize → Record → Reflect) provides a simple, memorable process:
1. **Recognize** a small success when it happens.
2. **Record** it (in a VSS journal, as illustrated).
3. **Reflect** on it to reinforce positive self-perception.

**Key reassurance:** The note "You don't have to share them" removes social pressure — this is a private, internal practice, lowering the barrier to entry.

**The VSS journal illustration** makes the abstract concrete: it shows a structured template for logging these small wins, numbered and organised, reinforcing that this is a real, actionable habit.

**WDTW (Win of the Week)** suggests a weekly review ritual — a higher-level reflection practice that aggregates daily small successes into a weekly highlight.

**The reflective question at the bottom** — *"Which Gremlin gets in the way of my work the most?"* — serves as a diagnostic prompt, encouraging the reader to identify their primary inner saboteur and target the practice accordingly.

The gremlin character inside the circular "cage" is deliberately cute and non-threatening, visually communicating that these inner critics, once recognised and contained, lose their power. The "Yehii" exclamation reinforces a tone of playful triumph.

Overall, the page communicates: *small, private, consistent wins are the antidote to self-sabotage.*
