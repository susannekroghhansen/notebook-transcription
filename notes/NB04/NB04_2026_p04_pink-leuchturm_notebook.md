## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page documents a **self-coaching exercise** structured around three interconnected activities:

**1. Strengths Identification (Top)**
The opening question — "What 3 strengths do you describe me with?" — is a classic coaching prompt asking the reader to consider how others perceive their strengths. The circled "3" emphasises the constraint of choosing just three, forcing prioritisation. The large question marks signal that this is an open, exploratory inquiry rather than a directive.

**2. Values Mapping via Energy Timeline (Middle)**
The "VALUES" section with the wavy line graph represents a **life/experience timeline exercise** — a common coaching tool where participants plot their emotional highs and lows over time or across activities. The labels "HIGH," "Low," and "Learning" suggest the axes or categories being tracked. The asterisks mark the most significant peaks and troughs. The instruction "WHAT DO HIGHS HAVE IN COMMON / DO IT IN PAIRS" indicates this is a paired or group coaching activity, where participants compare their highs to identify shared values or patterns.

**3. Reflection Framework (Bottom Left)**
The "WHAT? / SO WHAT? / NOW WHAT?" triad is a well-known **reflective learning model** (associated with John Dewey and later Driscoll's model of reflection). It structures experience into: describing what happened → interpreting its significance → deciding on future action. Its placement here suggests it is the analytical lens applied to the highs/lows exercise.

**4. Values Compass (Bottom Right)**
The compass rose with scored values (Achievement 8/10, Ideas 9/10, Learn 6/10, Variety 8/10) represents a **personal values assessment** — likely the output of the highs/lows analysis. The compass metaphor is apt: values act as a navigational system guiding decisions and behaviour. The scores suggest relative importance or current satisfaction with each value.

**Overall narrative:** The page presents a coherent self-coaching sequence — identify your strengths → map your highs and lows → reflect using a structured model → articulate your core values as a compass for direction. The diamond gems flanking "VALUES" visually reinforce the idea that values are precious, faceted, and worth examining closely.
