## 3. MEANING & INTERPRETATION

This page is a structured visual exploration of the concept of **personal strengths**, framed through the lens of **energy management**. The page appears to be notes from a strengths-based coaching or personal development framework (possibly referencing tools like CliftonStrengths or similar).

**Core argument of the page:**

The central metaphor is the **battery** — strengths are things that *charge* you (give you energy), and the page asks the reader to distinguish between **intent** (what you set out to do with your strengths) and **impact** (what actually happens as a result). The large battery with opposing arrows makes this tension vivid and immediate.

The **Energy Audit** section operationalises this idea: by tracking energy levels across each day of the week (M–F), the reader is invited to notice *when* they feel most energised — which is likely when they are using their strengths. The abbreviation "ea" (Energy Audit) repeated in each mini battery reinforces this as a daily practice.

The **"When → WHAT"** sequence in the middle of the page suggests a reflective methodology: first notice *when* you feel energised (temporal awareness), then investigate *what* you were doing in those moments (content awareness). This is a classic strengths identification approach.

The question **"How do I actively STRETCH my strengths?"** reframes strengths not as static traits but as capacities to be developed. The bottom bubble cluster — **Range, Share, Strategy, Frequency, Complementary** — offers five concrete dimensions for doing so:
- **Range:** Applying strengths in new contexts
- **Share:** Teaching or demonstrating strengths to others
- **Strategy:** Being intentional about deploying strengths
- **Frequency:** Using strengths more often
- **Complementary:** Pairing strengths with others' complementary strengths

The **lightbulb (ideas)** and **target (strategy)** icons reinforce the distinction between generative thinking and focused execution.

Overall, the page builds a coherent framework: *strengths give energy → audit your energy to find your strengths → notice when and what → actively stretch them across five dimensions.*
